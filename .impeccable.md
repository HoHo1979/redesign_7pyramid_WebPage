## Design Context

### Users
Wine buyers in Taiwan (Taipei Neihu, Hsinchu East District) spanning three segments: individual consumers browsing and purchasing premium wines, corporate clients seeking bulk orders and gifting services, and wine enthusiasts exploring knowledge and tasting guidance. Users arrive expecting a high-end, trustworthy merchant — not a marketplace or social platform. Primary language is Traditional Chinese with English secondary.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant, Trusted, Refined.** The voice is that of a knowledgeable sommelier — authoritative without being pretentious, warm without being casual. The brand bridges old-world wine heritage with a modern Taiwanese retail context. Seven Pyramid positions itself at the "$$$$" tier — this is not a discount shop.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial luxury** — think high-end print wine magazine, not a tech startup or generic e-commerce site. Light backgrounds (`#FFFFFF`, `#F6F3EE`, `#E8E0D4`) dominate for readability, with dark navy (`#07141E`, `#11324B`) and burgundy (`#800020` family) providing structure and warmth. Gold (`#D4AF37`) is reserved for premium highlights only — used sparingly for maximum impact.

**Reference**: The Wine Collective — restrained, editorial, curated.
**Anti-reference**: Glassmorphic SaaS sites, emoji-laden pages, centered-everything hero layouts, identical auto-fill grids.

**Typography**: Montserrat only (400–700 weights). No secondary fonts. Uppercase tracking reserved for category labels and navigation only.

**Depth**: Flat and restrained. Alternate background colors create rhythm — not shadows and bounding boxes. When elevation is needed, use warm tinted shadows (`rgba(10, 5, 8, 0.15)`), never pure black.

### Design Principles

1. **Editorial over template** — Layouts should feel curated like a print magazine: asymmetric, left-aligned, with varied content sizes. Never fall into identical repeating grids or textbook centered hero sections.

2. **Burgundy is the signature** — The burgundy palette (`#800020` through `#2A0A14`) is the most visible warm color on any page, bridging gold highlights and deep wine-red accents. It appears in headings, borders, hover states, and section backgrounds.

3. **Restraint signals luxury** — No glassmorphism, no emojis in structural text, no pure black, no heavy gradients or animations. Stillness and generous whitespace reflect confidence. Use 80px+ between major sections.

4. **Semantic over decorative** — Every color must come from CSS custom properties or MD3 tokens, never hard-coded inline hex. Every interactive element uses semantic HTML (`<button>`, `<a>`) with visible focus states.

5. **Accessibility is non-negotiable** — All interactive elements have `:focus-visible` outlines, 44x44px minimum touch targets on mobile, functional hamburger navigation, `prefers-reduced-motion` support, and no `div onclick` patterns.
