BURGUNDY

Architectural Heritage of Côte d'Or

Terroir, Limestone, & Monastic Origins

The geology of Burgundy is a complex mosaic of limestone and clay, a subterranean architecture meticulously charted by Cistercian and Benedictine monks over a thousand years ago. This heritage is not merely historical; it is a living blueprint for the most expressive Pinot Noir and Chardonnay on the planet.

In the Côte d'Or, the "Golden Slope," every plot or *climat* possesses a distinct atmospheric and mineral signature. Our archive focuses on the most prestigious crus—monolithic artifacts of viticulture that have remained unchanged in their boundaries since the Middle Ages.
