Questions & Insights

In the pursuit of the monolithic, clarity is the final vintage. We approach every inquiry with the same architectural precision we apply to our curation.

01 / Provenance & Authentication

How is the integrity of older vintages verified?

We employ a multi-layered verification protocol that begins with primary source documentation. Every bottle is examined for physical markers of authenticity, including capsule integrity, cork depth, and label typography consistent with the era of production.

For high-value monolithic selections, we cross-reference batch numbers directly with estate archives, ensuring a closed loop of custody from the cellar floor to our climate-controlled vault.

Can I request a detailed condition report?

Every allocation over a specific valuation is accompanied by a digital dossier. This includes high-resolution photography of the ullage levels, capsule condition, and any historical markings. We believe transparency is the foundation of institutional wine collecting.

"Preservation is not a static act, but a constant dialogue with time."

02 / The Procurement Process

What is the timeline for sourcing bespoke requests?

Sourcing exceptional wine is an architectural project, not a retail transaction. While our internal archive is vast, bespoke search mandates typically range from two to six weeks. This allows our global network of private collectors and estates to respond to our high standards for storage and provenance.

03 / Cellar Management

Do you offer advisory for physical cellar construction?

We consult on the environmental requirements necessary to house a monolithic collection. This includes thermal stability, vibration isolation, and light filtration strategies. We work alongside architects to ensure form meets the rigorous function of fine wine preservation.

How is valuation tracked over time?

Clients receive quarterly reports that benchmark their holdings against international auction performance and secondary market indices, providing a clear fiscal view of their liquid assets.
